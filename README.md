# Campaign-Management-Marketing
The focus of this project was to develop a high-performance, relevant, and well-illustrated dashboard for the data from a fictional marketing campaign, highlighting four different perspectives to facilitate a better understanding of the collected data.

The first dashboard, shown in the image above, displays the customer view, featuring key performance indicators (KPIs) based on the collected data. Below is an overview of the campaign’s customer trends. 

<img width="1152" height="644" alt="Captura de tela 2026-04-22 170042" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/71f84e06-7665-4fb6-80ec-b326f8ef0c27" />

The second dashboard displays an overview of the campaign's performance, with a focus on spending. I used a scatter plot to illustrate the relationship between total spending and annual salary. Based on this analysis, I concluded that the higher the salary, the higher the spending on general merchandise (such as food, electronics, toys, household items, etc.). 

Based on the hierarchical tree, I concluded that, in the scenario presented, single customers with a college degree are the ones who buy the most and spend the most on products. In addition, it is clear that spending on children is higher than on teenagers, which may suggest to the business team that the next marketing campaign should target couples who have recently become parents.
