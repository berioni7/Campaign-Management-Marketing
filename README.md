# Campaign-Management-Marketing
The focus of this project was to develop a high-performance, relevant, and well-illustrated dashboard for the data from a fictional marketing campaign, highlighting four different perspectives to facilitate a better understanding of the collected data.

The first dashboard, shown in the image above, displays the customer view, featuring key performance indicators (KPIs) based on the collected data. Below is an overview of the campaign’s customer trends. 

<img width="1152" height="644" alt="Captura de tela 2026-04-22 170042" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/71f84e06-7665-4fb6-80ec-b326f8ef0c27" />

The second dashboard displays an overview of the campaign's performance, with a focus on spending. I used a scatter plot to illustrate the relationship between total spending and annual salary. Based on this analysis, I concluded that the higher the salary, the higher the spending on general merchandise (such as food, electronics, toys, household items, etc.). 

Based on the hierarchical tree, I concluded that, in the scenario presented, single customers with a college degree are the ones who buy the most and spend the most on products. In addition, it is clear that spending on children is higher than on teenagers, which may suggest to the business team that they should better target their audience for future campaigns.

<img width="1146" height="643" alt="Captura de tela 2026-04-22 170056" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/65b86c56-c66e-4e07-a938-2a42c87df8b1" />

In the third slide, the charts show that the majority of the audience (singles) do not purchase the products featured in the campaign. This suggests that a future campaign may need to be more impactful in order to appeal to both singles and new parents. One example would be how overwhelming adult life can be, with both partners juggling work, school, and household responsibilities.

In the last dashboard, which features two slightly more complex charts, we can see which countries spend the most and how their spending has changed over time, based on the socioeconomic characteristics defined in the previous dashboards. It is clear that the U.S. has greater purchasing power, with its spending having grown the most over time.
